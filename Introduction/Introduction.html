The IBM 1620

The IBM 1620 was IBM's low-end, scientific computer in the early 1960s.

The IBM 1620 Project Team

The Computer History Museum's IBM 1620 team was formed in the Fall of 1998 with the initial goals of restoring the museum's IBM 1620 Model 1 computer to operation and collecting as much information and material on the IBM 1620 / 1710 / 1720 as possible. The project was successful and is documented here.

A subset of the team started a new project in 2016 to build a functional replica of the IBM 1620 using modern components, called the IBM 1620 Jr. That project is documented here.

The team members are: Dave Babcock [Team Leader], Charles Benner, Ken Cairns, Tim Casner, Mike Cheponis, Lee Courtney, Joe Fredrick, Richard Jowsey, Paul Kimpel, Don Krehbiel, Bill Maddox, Dr. John Maniotes, John Sansone, Len Shustek, Eric Smith, Dag Spicer, and David Wise.
