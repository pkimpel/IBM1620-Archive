    <div id=BodyTitle>IBM 1620 Census</div>

    <div id=BodyText>

        <p>The magazine "Computers and Automation" published a census of world-wide counts of installed computers every few months during the IBM 1620's lifetime.  The census was based on end-user surveys.  The counts were only as good as the surveys returned.  The census counts do not include the number of IBM 1620 computers that IBM had installed internally or any IBM 1710 / 1720 systems.  Other publications also published computer surveys, but on an irregular basis.  It's remarkable that so many government locations, companies, organizations, and schools would freely release information about what computers they had.</p>
